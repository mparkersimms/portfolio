About Me

Hi, my name is Matthew Simms. I have a background in Aviation and Leadership. I am an 11 year veteran of the United States Army. I've spent the last 6 years as Army Aviator, and the last two of those years as an Instructor Pilot for the CH-47F Chinook Helicopter.
  I decided to become a Software Developer because it's a field that I have had very limited knowledge on yet always found it enticing. I love to learn new things, be challenged, see growth and progress in myself, work in a team environment, and have a final product that showcases a specific challenge when completed. Software Development, as I have realized, fits that to the T.
I truly enjoy collaborating with other people toward a common goal and value the idea of using each others strengths together to build a team. I find myself thriving in small group settings, singularly focused, with an achievable goal that everyone can share in reaching, and truly feeling the rewards of that hard work and dedication.
 I have spent the last 11 years in the U.S. Army; three years as a Combat Medic, six years as a CH-47F Pilot-In-Command, and the last two years as an Instructor Pilot.
Major accomplishments and awards include: Completion of U.S. Army Ranger School; U.S. Army Flight School; Army Accommodation Medal with "V" device and "C" device.
The skills I've developed have been in Leadership, Team Work, Team Building, Project Management, Problem Solving, and Time Management. The time I have spent in the military has allowed me to develop a sense of grit and "stick-with-It-ness" that follows me into every aspect of my life; especially when it comes to challenges in the Tech industry. To me, the desired outcome of a given project or challenge IS the source of motivation, and my own internal drive is fueled by that motivation.  I am full of ambition and drive. I am creative. I am a leader, but also a follower. I am supportive, and I do not shy away from a challenge. I am very optimistic about the future, and look forward to the next step in my journey.
